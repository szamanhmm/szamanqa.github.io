1. Setting Up Closed Testing

Follow these essential steps to properly set up closed testing for your app and ensure it receives quality feedback before public release:

Create a Closed Testing Track

  • Log in to your Google Play Console
  • Navigate to Testing > Closed testing
  • Click on Create track
  • Enter a Track name (e.g., "Beta Testers") and click Create track

Select Countries/Regions

  • Go to the Countries/regions tab
  • Click on Add countries/regions
  • Select desired countries (e.g., USA, Canada, Bangladesh) or choose Select all countries/regions
  • Click Add

Add Testers

  • Navigate to the Testers tab
  • Under the "Testers" section, add testers via:
  • Email:
    • Click on Create email list
    • Enter a List name (e.g., "Team_Saifuz")
    • Add email addresses separated by commas or upload a CSV file
    • Click Save changes
  • Google Groups:
    • Select Google Groups
    • Enter the Google Group email addresses
    • Click Save changes

Prepare Your App Release

  • Go to Testing > Closed testing and select your created track
  • Click on Create new release
  • Upload your app bundle (AAB) or APK file
  • Provide release notes for your testers
  • Click Save

Roll Out the Release to Testers

  • Verify all information is correct
  • Click on Save and go to Publishing overview
  • Send the changes for review
  • Once approved, the app will be available to your testers

2. Tester Participation

Manage your testers effectively to gather valuable feedback and insights about your application:

Tester Onboarding

  • In the Testers tab of your closed testing track, copy the Opt-in URL
  • Share this link with your testers to allow them to opt-in and download your test app
  • Testers will follow the link, accept the invitation by clicking "Become a tester", and download from the Play Store

Testing Guidelines

  • Testers should use the app daily and provide comprehensive feedback
  • Each tester should use a unique Android device for more diverse testing
  • Continuous testing and feedback are essential for a successful transition to production

Tips for Successful Testing

  • Pre-Launch Reports:
    • Review pre-launch reports under Testing > Pre-launch report > Overview
    • Address issues related to stability, performance, and accessibility
  • Unique Devices:
    • Ensure testers use unique devices and test daily
    • Have testers explore as many app functionalities as possible
  • Monitor Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):
    • Enable KPIs in the dashboard (User acquisitions, Total installs, Audience growth rate)
    • Track installation counts and user growth during testing

3. Transitioning to Production

After successful testing, follow these steps to move your app to production and make it available to the public:

Apply for Production Access

  • After at least 14 days of continuous closed testing with a minimum of 12 testers (at least 14 recommended), apply for production
  • Go to your app's Dashboard in the Play Console
  • Click on Apply for production
  • Answer required questions about your closed test and app readiness
  • Submit your application and await approval (typically takes up to 1 to 3 days)
